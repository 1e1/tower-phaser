Chapter 2

Make it look good

A game that works is nice. A game that makes you want to play is better! Scenery, landscapes generated by maths, depth, particles and sounds.

1Scenery that changes: the biomes

Rather than drawing each scene by hand, we describe each “biome” with a small colour sheet. The game reads the sheet and paints everything on its own.

Four biomes: Meadow 🌳, Desert 🏜️, Tundra ❄️, Volcano 🌋. Same game, totally different mood: it's exactly what this notebook does, changing its outfit each chapter!

2A landscape made by maths

The terrain isn't drawn: it's computed. We add up several waves (sine curves, like the waves of the sea) of different sizes. The result: hills that look natural, and always different.

Try it! Generate new landscapes. The “roughness” slider makes the hills smoother or more rugged (like the desert or the volcano!).

Landscape generator

Same random “seed” = same landscape. That's what lets the TV and the phones show exactly the same terrain (chapter 3!).

3Depth: parallax

To give a sense of depth, we stack layers that move at different speeds: the far clouds drift slowly, the foreground faster. Your brain understands “ah, that's far / that's near”. It's called parallax.

4Living particles

A particle is a tiny dot that is born, moves, changes size/transparency, then dies. On their own they're nothing; by the hundreds, they make the smoke, the sparks, the snow, the embers, the dust.

  • 💥 on firing: a muzzle flash + sparks;
  • 🌫️ on impact: dust that falls back down;
  • 🍃 in the background: leaves, sand, snow or embers depending on the biome, pushed by the wind.
For the curious. We don't create each snowflake by hand! We describe rules (speed, lifetime, start and end size) and the engine makes hundreds of particles that follow those rules.

5Sound… without any audio file

Surprising: the game contains no sound file at all. Every sound effect is made on the fly by code, with Web Audio. An explosion? Some “noise” (like the static of an old TV) that we filter and fade out very fast.

Try it! Turn your device's volume up a little, then click. (The sound effects need a click to start: browsers forbid automatic sound.)
